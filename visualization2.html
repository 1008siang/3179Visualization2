The first thought in mind when we talk about the population in Asia will be India and China which having the highest population .
However, these 2 countries both has a large land area of 9,388,211 kilometer square (China) and 2,973,190 kilometer square (India).
Hence, instead of using the population,the population density will be more preferable to be considered as a normalized attribute.
So, when we observe the choropleth map above, Taiwan and Lebanon are the contries having the highest density in Asia .
The most obvious similarity between them is that, both of the countires have small land area and high population. (Click on the map to observe the area. )

The most common factor when people talk about population or population density of a country is the fertility rate.
Does higher fertility rate indicates higher population density ? The answer may be a NO .

In this case, the map above shows that Afghanistan has the highest fertility rate among the countires in Asia.
However, the population density of the country belongs to the lowest threshold of below 200 (people/kilometer square).
Besides, the 2 countries which having the highest density,Taiwan and Lebanon only has the fertility rate of 1.2 and 2.1.
(Click on the map to observe the area and the fertility rate. )

Hence, we can actually conclude that, the population density of a specific country was not affected by the fertility rate
However, in the other cas that the fertility rate does affect the median age of a country.

The most obvious example will be pointed towards Japan which has a low fertility rate of 1.4 as ratio between the number of live births
in a year and the whole female population of childbearing age. In other words, the number of children in the country will be fewer.
Hence, the median age of the country will then be higher and reaches 48 which is the highest median age in Asia.

The scatter plot will then better visualize how the fertility rate of a country can affects the median age.
